🔋 Battery health

How this works
Normalized rated range = EstBatteryRange × 100 / Soc. This estimates what the car's range would be at 100% charge, regardless of current SOC. Watching this value over months reveals real degradation, separated from day-to-day noise.

To keep the line clean, samples are filtered: SOC must be ≥ 30%, and the car must not have been in Drive/Reverse within ±5 min. Daily median is plotted as a solid line; individual filtered samples appear as faint dots.

How this works
Efficiency % = rated consumption ÷ actual consumption × 100. Above 100% means you beat the car's rated Wh/mi; below means worse (cold, heat/AC, high speeds, or short trips all hurt). Drives are grouped into 10°F buckets using the car's own OutsideTemp sensor. Energy is derived from SOC change, so very short drives are noisy — buckets get more reliable as you log more miles.
